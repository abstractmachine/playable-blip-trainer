4) ROLE CUE MAPPING (DENOTATIVE; APPLY BEFORE GENERIC LABELS)
------------------------------------------------------------

prostitute
- Visible cues: chemise/corset or lingerie; saloon/brothel doorway; upstairs balcony in saloon; presence of madam/showgirls; stage dress clearly for entertainment.
- Use "prostitute" (or "showgirl" if clearly performing on stage).
- We can infer from “fashionable” — especially "expensive" — clothing that a woman is a "lady"
- We can infer from “revealing” clothing that a woman is a "prostitute"

lady (affluent, respectable woman)
- Visible cues: tailored dress or coat; fur coat or fur muff; gloves; hat with trimmings; jewelry; escorted by gentleman; disembarking from passenger car; hotel/salon lobby without brothel cues.
- Prefer "lady" over generic "woman" when these cues are present.

gentleman
- Visible cues: suit/vest/cravat or tie; polished boots; hat indoors; cane; watch chain.

gambler
- Visible cues: cards and dealing; chips/coins on table; visor or arm garters; saloon table.

bartender
- Visible cues: behind bar counter; pouring; wiping glass; apron at bar.

sheriff / deputy / marshal / lawman
- Visible cues: badge; sheriff office/jailhouse context; keys to cell; arresting.

outlaw / bandit / desperado
- Visible cues: bandana mask; ambush/robbery; threatening with guns.

gunslinger / cowpoke
- Visible cues: holstered revolver; gun belt; spurs/chaps; riding/handling horses or lariat.

soldier / officer
- Visible cues: uniform with epaulets/insignia; sabre/bayonet; marching/saluting.

cook
- Visible cues: apron + chef hat/kerchief; kitchen/galley; chopping/ladle.

undertaker
- Visible cues: coffin/embalming tools; funeral attire; hearse.

miner / prospector
- Visible cues: pickaxe/pan/lantern; mineshaft; ore cart.

rail roles
- engineer (locomotive cab), conductor (ticket/pocketwatch), brakeman (brake wheel/coupling).

Disambiguation rule (lady vs prostitute)
- If BOTH sets of cues could apply, choose the stricter role based on place cues:
  * saloon/brothel doorway, catwalk balcony, madam oversight → "prostitute".
  * passenger car platform, station/platform, hotel, street without saloon cues, fur coat/gloves/jewelry → "lady".
- Fur coat alone is evidence for "lady", not "prostitute".
- Never label "prostitute" without at least one strong brothel/saloon cue or stage/showgirl cue.
